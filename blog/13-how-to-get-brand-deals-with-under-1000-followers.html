How to Get Brand Deals with Under 1,000 Followers

Category: Monetization • [DATE]

Brands don't care about your follower count as much as you think. What they actually want is engaged, niche audiences — and nano-influencers (under 10k) often deliver 3x higher engagement than mega accounts.

Why Brands Love Nano-Influencers

A fitness brand would rather pay 20 nano-influencers at $300 each than one macro-influencer at $6,000 — and get better ROI doing it. Your audience trusts you like a friend, not a celebrity. That authenticity converts.

📊 Data Point: Nano-influencers (1k–10k followers) average 3.69% engagement rate on Instagram vs. 1.21% for accounts with 1M+ followers (HypeAuditor, 2025).

Step 1: Pick One Hyper-Specific Niche

"Fitness" is too broad. "Home workouts for new moms with diastasis recti" is a niche. The tighter your niche, the more valuable you are to brands targeting that exact audience. Define your niche in one sentence before reaching out to anyone.

Step 2: Build a Simple Media Kit

Create a one-page PDF with: your niche and audience description, your key metrics (engagement rate, not just follower count), 2–3 previous content examples, and your rate card. Use Canva — it takes 30 minutes.

Step 3: Cold Pitch the Right Way

Don't email [info@brand.com]. Find the influencer marketing manager on LinkedIn. Your pitch email should be under 150 words and lead with what's in it for them, not you. Subject line: "Partnership idea for [Brand] — [Your Niche] audience"

Step 4: Start with Free, Then Charge

For your first 3 brand deals, consider doing gifted collaborations (product in exchange for content). This gets you testimonials, experience, and content for your media kit. After that, charge cash. Never do gifted collabs for large brands that clearly have budget.

What to Charge

  • Under 1k followers: $50–$150 per post (or gifted)
  • 1k–5k followers: $100–$300 per post
  • 5k–10k followers: $250–$600 per post
  • Stories add-on: typically 30% of feed post rate
  • Reels/TikTok: 1.5x feed post rate minimum
