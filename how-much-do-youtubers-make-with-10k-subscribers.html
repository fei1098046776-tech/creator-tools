How Much Do YouTubers Make with 10k Subscribers? (2026 Data)

Published April 27, 2026 • 5 min read

Hitting 10,000 subscribers is a major milestone for any YouTube creator. It’s the point where your channel starts looking established, you have a solid core audience, and brands start taking you seriously.

But the burning question remains: How much money does a 10k subscriber channel actually make?

The short answer? Between $100 to $2,500 per month. That is a massive range, and it exists because subscriber count is actually the least important metric for income. Let's break down the real numbers based on our 2026 creator data study.

1. AdSense Revenue (YouTube Partner Program)

Once you pass 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours, you get accepted into YPP. Your AdSense revenue depends entirely on two factors: Views and RPM (Revenue Per Mille).

  • Low RPM Niches (Gaming, Pranks): $1 - $3 per 1,000 views.
  • Medium RPM Niches (Tech, Lifestyle, Fitness): $4 - $8 per 1,000 views.
  • High RPM Niches (Finance, Business, Software): $12 - $30+ per 1,000 views.

If your 10k sub channel gets 50,000 views a month in a tech niche (avg $6 RPM), you will make around $300/month just from YouTube ads.

2. Brand Sponsorships

At 10,000 subscribers, you are officially a "Micro-Influencer." Brands love micro-influencers because their audiences are highly engaged and trust their recommendations.

In 2026, the standard rate is roughly $20 to $30 per 1,000 average views. If your videos average 5,000 views, you can charge about $100 - $150 per integrated ad read. Doing 4 of these a month adds another $600 to your income.

3. Affiliate Marketing

This is often the hidden goldmine. By placing Amazon affiliate links or software referral links in your description, you earn a cut of any purchases. Tech reviewers with 10k subs easily make $200 - $500/month in passive affiliate income just by linking the gear they use.

The Final Verdict

A highly optimized, business-focused YouTube channel with 10k subscribers can easily generate a full-time income of $2,000+ a month. However, a general vlog or gaming channel with the same subscriber count might only make $100.

To maximize your worth, focus on increasing your RPM and negotiating better brand deals. Stop guessing your value—use the data.
