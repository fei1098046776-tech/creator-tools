What Is a Good Instagram Engagement Rate in 2025?

Instagram's average engagement rate dropped significantly between 2020 and 2025 as the platform grew. Today, the platform average is approximately 1.94% for feed posts. Carousels perform 3x better than single images on average, while Reels can see wildly variable results depending on the For You distribution.

A good Instagram engagement rate depends heavily on follower count. Nano-influencers (under 10K) typically see 5–8% ER, while celebrities with 10M+ followers may see rates below 0.5%.

Instagram Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Follower Count

  • Under 10K (Nano): 5–8% average, >8% is excellent
  • 10K–100K (Micro): 2–5% average, >5% is excellent
  • 100K–500K (Mid-Tier): 1–3% average, >3.5% is excellent
  • 500K–1M (Macro): 0.5–1.5% average, >2% is excellent
  • 1M+ (Mega): 0.2–0.8% average, >1% is excellent

Why Saves Are the Most Important Instagram Metric

Instagram's 2025 algorithm weighs saves more heavily than likes or comments as a signal of content quality. A post with 100 saves tells the algorithm that viewers found the content valuable enough to return to, triggering broader distribution. If you want to grow, focus on creating content that people will save — tutorials, checklists, infographics, and inspirational content outperform entertainment in saves.

FAQ

How do brands calculate Instagram sponsorship rates?
Most brands use a CPM model: $10–$20 per 1,000 followers as a baseline, then adjust up or down based on engagement rate, niche relevance, and exclusivity. A micro-influencer with 50K followers and 5% ER will typically earn $200–$500 per post.
Does Instagram show engagement rate in analytics?
Instagram Professional Dashboard shows reach, impressions, and interactions but doesn't calculate engagement rate directly. Use this calculator to compute it manually from your post stats.
Do Reels have higher engagement rates than photos?
Reels often generate more views but lower engagement rates as a percentage of followers, because many views come from non-followers who are less likely to interact. For brand deals, feed posts and carousels often show better "engaged audience" metrics.
