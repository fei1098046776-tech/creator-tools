How Much Does YouTube Pay Per 1,000 Views in 2025?

YouTube pays creators through its Partner Program (YPP) using a metric called RPM (Revenue Per Mille) — the amount you earn per 1,000 views after YouTube takes its 45% cut. In 2025, the average YouTube RPM across all niches ranges from $1.50 to $18, depending heavily on your channel category and audience location.

Finance channels consistently see the highest RPMs ($12–$18), because advertisers in that niche pay premium rates to reach high-intent audiences. Gaming and entertainment channels typically earn less ($1.50–$4) but can compensate with massive view counts.

YouTube RPM by Niche — 2025 Data

  • Finance & Investing: $12–18 RPM
  • Business & Marketing: $8–12 RPM
  • Technology & Reviews: $5–8 RPM
  • Education & How-To: $4–7 RPM
  • AI & Productivity: $3–6 RPM
  • Gaming: $2–4 RPM
  • Entertainment / Vlogs: $1.5–3 RPM

How to Increase Your YouTube Earnings

1. Shift toward high-RPM topics

Even occasional finance or business-adjacent videos can boost your channel's average RPM significantly. Creators in general lifestyle niches often increase RPM by 40–60% by adding videos about budgeting, side income, or career topics.

2. Target US, UK, Canadian audiences

YouTube ad rates are dramatically higher in English-speaking markets. A channel with 100K views from the US might earn 5–10x more than the same views from South Asia or Southeast Asia.

3. Enable all ad formats

Enable skippable ads, non-skippable ads, bumper ads, overlay ads, and sponsored cards. Creators who enable all formats report 20–35% higher RPM than those using only default settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many views do you need to make $1,000/month on YouTube?
At an average RPM of $5, you'd need roughly 200,000 monetized views per month. With a higher-RPM niche like finance ($14 RPM), you'd only need about 71,000 views. The key lever is niche, not just views.
What is the difference between RPM and CPM on YouTube?
CPM (Cost Per Mille) is what advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions. RPM is what you actually receive per 1,000 views after YouTube takes its 45% revenue share. So if CPM is $10, your RPM will be approximately $5.50.
Do YouTube Shorts earn the same as regular videos?
No. YouTube Shorts RPM is significantly lower — typically $0.03–$0.08 per 1,000 views. YouTube pools ad revenue from Shorts into a creator fund and distributes it differently. Long-form videos remain far more lucrative per view.
When does YouTube pay you?
YouTube pays monthly, typically between the 21st–26th of each month, for earnings from the previous month. You need a minimum threshold of $100 in your AdSense account before payment is released.
