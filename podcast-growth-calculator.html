How Much Can Podcasts Make from Advertising?

Podcast advertising uses a CPM model (cost per 1,000 downloads). In 2025, podcast CPM rates range from $10 to $50 depending on niche, audience demographics, and ad placement. Business and finance podcasts command the highest CPMs because their listeners are high-income decision-makers that advertisers prize.

Most podcasts need at least 5,000–10,000 downloads per episode to attract programmatic advertising. Below that threshold, direct sponsorships from brands in your niche are often more lucrative.

Podcast Download Milestones and What They Mean

  • 0–1,000 downloads/episode: Grow your audience first. Focus on quality, consistency, and word-of-mouth.
  • 1,000–5,000 downloads/episode: Start direct outreach to niche sponsors. Rates: $15–$50 CPM.
  • 5,000–20,000 downloads/episode: Qualify for most podcast ad networks. Top 20% of all podcasts.
  • 20,000–100,000 downloads/episode: Top 5% of podcasts. Premium sponsorship rates available.
  • 100,000+ downloads/episode: Top 1%. Multiple six-figure annual ad revenue possible.

Best Podcast Ad Networks for Monetization

The largest podcast ad networks include Spotify Audience Network, iHeartMedia, Acast, Megaphone, and Podbean Ads Marketplace. Qualification requirements vary, but most require a minimum of 1,000–2,000 downloads per episode. For smaller podcasts, platforms like Podcorn and Anchor allow direct brand matching without minimum download thresholds.

FAQ

How many listeners do you need to make money podcasting?
Technically zero — even small podcasters can do affiliate marketing or sell their own products. But for traditional CPM-based ad revenue, most networks require 2,000–5,000 downloads per episode. Direct brand deals can start even at 500–1,000 loyal listeners if your niche is specific enough.
What's a good monthly growth rate for a new podcast?
For new podcasts in their first year, 5–15% monthly growth is strong. This compounds significantly: 8% monthly growth means you'll roughly double every 9 months. Podcasts that invest in YouTube clips, social sharing, and cross-promotions often see 15–25% monthly growth.
Pre-roll vs. mid-roll ads — which pays more?
Mid-roll ads command 2–3x the CPM of pre-roll ads because listeners are already engaged and less likely to skip. Pre-roll CPM is typically $10–$15, while mid-roll ranges from $20–$40 for the same audience.
