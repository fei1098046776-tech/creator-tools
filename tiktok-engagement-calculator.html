What is a Good TikTok Engagement Rate?

TikTok consistently has the highest engagement rates of any social platform. While Instagram averages around 1–3% engagement, TikTok averages 4.1% across all creator sizes. This is largely because TikTok's algorithm surfaces content based on interest signals, not just follower relationships.

A good TikTok engagement rate in 2025 is anything above 4%. Rates above 8% are considered excellent and will attract top-tier brand deal offers.

How Brands Use Engagement Rate

When brands evaluate creators for sponsorships, engagement rate is often more important than raw follower count. A creator with 50K followers and 8% ER will typically earn more per post than a creator with 200K followers at 1.5% ER. This is why calculating your true engagement rate matters before any brand negotiation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is TikTok engagement rate calculated?
TikTok ER = (Total Interactions ÷ Followers) × 100. Total interactions include likes, comments, and shares per video. We recommend calculating the average over your last 10–20 videos for accuracy.
Does TikTok views count as engagement?
Views are not included in standard engagement rate calculations because views are passive. Brands focus on active interactions — likes, comments, shares, and saves — as these indicate real audience investment.
Why is my TikTok engagement rate dropping?
Common causes: posting frequency changes, niche drift (content no longer matching audience expectations), or algorithmic shifts. Analyze your last 30 videos to identify which content types drive the most interaction.
